How has Pixar's gender ratio changed over the last 20 years?

How has the number of women in each department changed over time?

Learn More

In 2009, 158.6 million women were living in the United States. Women make up more than 51% of the U.S. work force; however, of the 3,816,000 computer-related occupations in the country, only 26% are filled by women. Influenced by these numbers, we decided it would be interesting to see the percentage of women employed for the Pixar films throughout the years. We found unexpected trends from the data we gathered. Look at the graphs and see if you can find them as well.

However, it is important to note that all of our data is sourced from IMDB, which relies on self reporting, and not all departments are correlated to official Pixar departments.

IMDB does not provide the gender of crew members, so we used an API called genderize to determine their gender. This API is approximately 90% accurate, so there may be some errors in the data.
